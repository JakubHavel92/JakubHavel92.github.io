The Questionable
History of Our Café

Legend has it that this café was founded in 1732 by a traveling clockmaker who got lost, ran out of time (literally), and decided to start brewing coffee instead. According to several highly unreliable sources, the original building was constructed overnight by a group of extremely motivated pigeons, who were paid in breadcrumbs and compliments. The café quickly became a meeting place for philosophers, poets, and at least one confused goat who wandered in and was never asked to leave.

Over the years, the café has survived minor inconveniences such as three imaginary fires, a brief takeover by a society of dramatic squirrels, and a complete redesign inspired by a dream someone’s cousin had after eating too much cheese. Today, we proudly continue this entirely unverifiable tradition—serving coffee that is almost certainly real, pastries that may or may not obey the laws of physics, and stories that are best enjoyed with a healthy dose of skepticism.
